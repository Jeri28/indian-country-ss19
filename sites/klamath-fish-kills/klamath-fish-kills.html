Introduction

Fishing marine resources along the Klamath river is an important aspect in the lifeways of the tribes along the Klamath including the Yurok, Kalmath, Shasta, Karuk and Hoppa tribes. The river has been sustaining the indigenous people's living on it's banks for many generations. Hpwever, in the past hundred years the Klamath River has become a hot spot for commerical fishing and new industry. The overfishing, pollution and dam construction along the Klmath River has caused a devestation to the rivers marine life, most recently a major fish kill of salmon along the river in 2002 and 2018. This site takes an in depth look into the exact causes and consequences of the fish kills along the Klamath River as well as looking at the case study of how the long standing Yurok tribe is federally combating the issue today.
